    How your army works in real scenarios.

    Here's what it looks like when you give a single command and your entire agent army coordinates to execute it.

    "Launch the new product campaign"

    You tell your Orchestrator. It activates your Marketing CEO to draft content and schedule posts, your Sales CEO to set up lead capture sequences, your Operations CEO to create project milestones, and your Finance CEO to set a campaign budget tracker. All from one sentence.

    Agents involved: Orchestrator → Marketing → Sales → Operations → Finance

    "What's our financial position this month?"

    Your Orchestrator routes this to the Finance CEO, which pulls invoice data, expense reports, and revenue figures — then compiles a clean summary and sends it back to you in seconds. No spreadsheets. No digging.

    Agents involved: Orchestrator → Finance

    "A customer is unhappy — handle it"

    The Orchestrator sends this to your Customer Success CEO, which pulls up the ticket history, drafts an empathetic response, escalates if needed, and logs the interaction. Your Sales CEO is also notified to flag the account for retention risk.

    Agents involved: Orchestrator → Customer Success → Sales

    "Help me think through this acquisition"

    This goes straight to your Thought Assistant — which researches the target company, analyzes financials, identifies risks, drafts a pro/con framework, and helps you model different outcomes. Pure strategy, on demand.

    Agents involved: Orchestrator → Thought Assistant
